Drawing Machine

Project Description

For the same design innovation class, I created a light-powered drawing machine made with acrylic gears rotating a sharpie. Demo Video

Process

In my first iteration, I laser cut wooden gears and tried to draw somethingÂ by rotating the smaller gears by hand. One of the arms was longer than the other in an attempt to create a more interesting shape, but the gear ratio for both smaller gears to big gear was the same, so it didn't work out too well.

In the second iteration, I changed the number of teeth on the smaller gears to play with the gear ratio. I also added a third smaller gear to turn the gear underneath the plate more easily. The arms extended and got stuck at their furthest points from the other arm on the gears, so this had to be fixed.

The wood gears ultimately added too much friction to the rotating motion of the arms. Acrylic gears worked much better.

I also designed a motor housing and a part to connect the motor to the dowel that turns the gears on top.

I used a photoresistor and Arduino to power my drawing machine. Different amounts of light correspond to different drawing speeds (more light, faster speed).
